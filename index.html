Waterloop
Levitation Interm Lead,
Embedded IMU Sub-team Lead

My experience at Waterloop was an unforgettable one. The amount of late nights that were invested into this amazing project was worth every second.

Waterloop is the University of Waterloo’s Hyperloop team for the SpaceX Competition in California. There are 2 phases to the competition. The first phase is the design proposal, which only 30 teams of over 1400 are selected to move forward into production of the pod. The second phase is the creation of a half-scale Hyperloop pod. Once I caught wind of the competition, I knew I had to join in and be a part of the experience. I was able to see my contributions to the electrical levitation circuit and the Inertial Measurement Unit which were embedded directly into the pod.

It’s easy to get caught up in the hype of such an amazing competition. What makes the experience so special are the students you are working with throughout the process. Together, you are representing the university and student design teams on an international level, and putting your valuable skills to use. The amount of information I learned through Waterloop was incredible – and it was free! I will post an update with the results of the competition in February. Until then, stay tuned!
